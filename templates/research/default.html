Wuchna.com is our effort to collate the world's real-time data into one hub, power it by AI and and letting it serve humanity (and investors). The idea is to have a Wiki without human authors, and with videos and ads. Wuchna is designed to be Wikipedia 2.0 but with a different take on tech, cash and management philosophies.


Wikipedia.org is the world's 5th most visited website today. We here at Wuchna are VERY VERY grateful to its contribution to humanity and the growth of the web. But we intend to disrupt its model, since its time has come. The new generation relies on videos, has very little attention span, and the information world has spread too much far across and wide to make a manual effort at collating this information possible. A large part of the online world now resides in Asia and Africa, but the wiki world is yet to reach them. In today's world, it is indeed a bad idea to write down the world's information by hand: information is growing at a faster rate than our hands ever could write.

A child outgrowing a parent does not diminish or threaten the value of the parent. Rather it becomes the heritage of the child to preserve the values and the legacy of the parent, while also embibing the elements of the new era: the new economy, the new society, the new tech, the new management and the shorter attention spans of the new generation. In our humble opinion, the primary deficits of Wikipedia.org model are: reliance on human editing, selection of topics, no monetization, old design in a new world and bad user engagement. On the other hand, it is the largest and most trusted hub of content that humanity has ever known. What could a child of such a parent build upon and enrich the world with?

If an image is worth a thousand words, a video could turn out to be infinte words?? AI is coming up. News and business topics need to be integrated into the site. Social feeds could be integrated, and people should be able to ask questions and get answers from the community. The design could be enhanced upon, user engagement can be better, and all of this is possible if monetization is not ignored. The new world focuses on sharing, and we need to leverage the possibilities to create something which is much larger than the individual parts. This is our vision for the future: better content mix, more engagement, more topics, and proper management.
